# Building an Industrial AI Company — 5 Pillars

> How to build the next Algo8 / PlantBrain from scratch

---

## Pillar 1 — Domain Expertise 🏭
**Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Hardest**

You cannot build this from a laptop. You need people who have spent years inside manufacturing plants.

**What you need:**
- Process engineers with 10+ years plant floor experience
- Plant operations veterans who know failure patterns
- Industry advisors with existing client relationships
- Access to a pilot customer and their sensor data

> **Why it matters:** Without domain knowledge, your AI models will be technically correct and operationally useless. Plant managers won't trust a system that doesn't speak their language.

---

## Pillar 2 — Technical Team 💻
**Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ Medium**

Not 50 people. The right 8–12 to start.

**Core hires:**
- ML engineers specialising in time-series
- Data engineers for IoT pipelines
- Backend / cloud infrastructure
- Frontend engineers for dashboards
- 1 "domain + tech" hybrid (rarest hire)

> **Key insight:** The hardest hire is someone who understands both the ML model and what happens inside a rotary kiln. They exist — but they're rare.

---

## Pillar 3 — A Lighthouse Customer 🎯
**Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Hard**

Get a paying pilot customer **before** you build the full product.

**What this customer gives you:**
- Real sensor data to train models on
- A real operational problem to solve
- A case study to sell your second customer
- Feedback that shapes the actual product

**What to look for:**
- Mid-size manufacturer (not too big, not too small)
- A champion inside the organisation
- Willingness to share plant floor access
- Pain point that is measurable (downtime, quality, energy)

> **The rule:** You cannot build Industrial AI in a vacuum. The first customer is not a revenue event — it is a product development event.

---

## Pillar 4 — Capital 💰
**Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ Medium**

Industrial AI has long sales cycles (6–18 months). You need runway.

| Stage | Amount | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Pre-seed | $100K–$500K | Founder salaries + first pilot |
| Seed | $500K–$2M | MVP + first 3 deployments |
| Series A | $5M–$15M | Scale team + 10+ customers |

**Key cost drivers:**
- Deployment & integration at each plant site
- Enterprise sales cycle — no quick closes
- Maintaining models per client (each plant is different)

---

## Pillar 5 — Go-to-Market Network 🤝
**Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Hard**

Industrial companies don't buy from LinkedIn ads.

**How they buy:**
- Referrals from trusted peers
- Industry conferences (ACHEMA, CII, Hannover Messe)
- Government / PSU relationships (India-specific)
- System integrator channel partners

**What you need on day one:**
- 1 founder or advisor with an existing Rolodex
- Presence at 1–2 industry events per year
- A reference customer willing to talk publicly

> **The trust problem:** Getting a plant manager to connect your AI to a ₹500 crore asset takes 6–18 months of relationship building — not a product demo.

---

## What You Can Buy vs Must Build

| Can Buy / Hire | Must Build Yourself |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Cloud infra (AWS/Azure) | Domain-specific AI models |
| ML frameworks (PyTorch, sklearn) | Plant integration connectors |
| IoT middleware (MQTT) | Operator UX (plant-floor grade) |
| Dashboard tools | Trust with industrial clients |
| Sales & marketing talent | Deployment methodology |

---

## The Workflow

```
Step 1 — Find domain expert + lighthouse customer
         ↓
Step 2 — Hire core tech team (8–12 people)
         ↓
Step 3 — Build MVP on real customer data (3–6 months)
         ↓
Step 4 — Raise seed round using pilot results
         ↓
Step 5 — Use first case study to land 2nd customer
         ↓
Step 6 — Productise learnings into platform (PlantBrain)
         ↓
Step 7 — Build GTM network + channel partners
         ↓
Step 8 — Series A → scale to 10+ deployments
```
